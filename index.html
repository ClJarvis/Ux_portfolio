Medicine Minder

A Case Study

I worked on a application to notify users when that their prescriptions were running low, so they could reorder before they ran out.

Interviews: Example Questions with Aggregated answers

I interviewed potential users to see if there was a need for my product idea. Interviews were done in person or via email. I complied the responses to get a better feel for what my product needed.

    Sample Survey Questions
  1. Have you ever ran or almost ran out of a prescription?
    • Yes, happens often,
  2. How do You feel if you run out?
    • Annoyed, Upset, Stupid, Worried, Irresponsible
  3. What would help?
    • Text, Push Notification, email

Persona

I complied the interviews to create a Persona or primary user. Primary user is someone that is tired of running out of her family's prescriptions. She is busy juggling life, work, kids and needs an easy way to track the level of family prescriptions.

Jayne Berry

Persona

  • Age Bracket: 35-44
  • Occupation: QA Analyst
  • Family: Married, kids
  • Persona: End User

Goals

  1. Easy & Clear understaning of Pharmacy needs
  2. To keep her family healthy.
  3. Spend time with kids after work.
  4. Reduce paper work

Technology Preferences

  1. Prefers Text over Phone Calls
  2. Automate it!
  3. E-receipt over paper

Quote

"I don't often have the time to count out the pills I have left."

Journey Map

The steps user goes through daily. Jayne just noticed that she is low on pills and is worried that she won't get her refills in time. Once she has the app, she can chose how many days in advance to be alerted. She now gets an auto alert. It makes her happy that she can now call her pharmacy and get an refill order placed in advance.

Wireframe

I sketched a basic prototype in a note book. It took several iterations to get everything I wanted on the design. Then I showed to some people got some feedback then I moved on to a wire frame.

basic entry form to collect prescription information. I thought it would be easy to manually enter in the information. But after talking to a couple users, it was suggested to use the phone to scan the info off a bottle or receipt.

Prototype

I built a wire frame using a prototyping tool, UXpin. The interactive prototype allowed users to experience the proposed functions of the app. I was able to get feedback in person and online. The feedback helped to refine my concept.

basic entry form to collect prescription information. User can check her Medicince cabinet and witha glance know how many pills she has left of all of her perscriptions. And which ones will be running out soon so she can reorder mutliple scriptions at once.

Case Drawing

The user notices she is out of pills. She forgot to call in a refill and will now have to wait two days before see can get a new supply. With Medicine Minder She gets a text reminder two days before her last pill. She can tap a button to call her Pharmacy and request a refill. She can pick up her medicine without having to wait.
